Common Myths about Cats

Despite their popularity in many households, numerous myths and misconceptions float around in common culture. Here's some ideas which you might here tossed around, and why they're wrong.

Myth Fact
Cats are low-maintenance pets. Cats require just as much care and attention as any other animal. They require play, a proper diet, medical care, and socialization to the same degree that a dog does. A bored and lonely cat may be prone to anxiety, destruction, and restlessness.
Cats are bad luck. Owning a cat or dog is linked to decreased blood pressure, lower stress, and less anxiety and depression. Additionally, cats purr at a frequency that can improve bone growth and healing.
Cats are mean. Cats have specific body language that cannot be equated to human or dog behavior. Some cats are more easily overstimulated than others, and may lash out when stressed.
Cats can be vegan. All cats are obligate carnivores. Refusing to give a pet its proper diet is animal cruelty. Without meat, cats can experience cardiomyopathy and blindness. Consider instead looking for brands with humane farming practices, or buying a pet that can thrive off of vegetarian diets, such as rabbits, some rodents, or herbivorous fish.
Cats can have milk. A little bit of milk won't hurt your cat, but most cats are lactose intolerant, and may experience upset stomachs, vomiting, and diarrhea. In general, you should always research whether something is toxic before letting your pet try it.
